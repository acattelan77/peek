Peek Design System
Menu-bar calendar companion for macOS · v1.1

A calm, glance-first system built on native macOS materials with one confident accent. Every surface answers a single question fast — what’s next, and how do I join it?

01 Direction
Glanceable first
Title, time, and join action outrank everything else. Metadata recedes to a quiet second layer.
One accent per row
A single Peek Blue drives focus. Urgency borrows amber & red only when time demands it.
Native, not neutral
System materials, focus rings, and SF typography — with just enough personality to feel like Peek.

11 App icon, refreshed

Flatter and cleaner than the rendered original: a squircle tile, a calendar page with a peeled corner, and one coral “next” slot. It keeps the fold and the single highlight while dropping heavy 3D shading so it reads at 16 px. Below is a build target — render the final master in a design tool at full resolution.

Rules that carry over
Keep the page fold, the single coral slot, and the indigo → midnight field. Never add text, numbers, a dense month grid, or window traffic lights. Verify legibility at 16, 32, 128, and 1024 px after every change.

15 Content & voice

Peek speaks in plain, calm, present tense. It states the fact and the next action — never nags, never jokes about being late.

Do
“Starting in 2 min”
“You’re all clear”
“Calendar access needed”
“in 12 min” · “in 3 hr” · “10:30 AM”
Don’t
“Hurry! You’re about to be late!”
“Nothing here 😴”
“Oops, we can’t see your calendar”
“720 seconds until start”
RuleApplied
Title truncationTwo lines max, then ellipsis — time & join never clip
Relative vs absoluteRelative under 1 hr (“in 12 min”), clock time beyond
CapitalizationSentence case everywhere; ALL-CAPS only for 9 pt status labels
